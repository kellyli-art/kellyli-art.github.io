about me

    For inquiries or collaborations, please contact me by email at kellyli2016@gmail.com.

Kelly Li is a first-year student at Harvard Medical School. Her love for the creative arts took root in early childhood, when she was trained in traditional media such as charcoal, watercolor, and acrylics. By teaching at a local cultural school, she discovered how art can bridge cultures and build connection. During undergrad, she sought to bring this healing power to patient bedsides through the art programs in the Yale-New Haven Pediatric Emergency Department and Connecticut Hospice.

To apply creativity to advance humanity in other ways, she studied biomedical engineering at Yale University. After graduating, she joined a biotechnology start-up to develop engineered heart tissue technology, and continued cultivating her creativity to design new solutions within medicine.

Now, she strives to continue growing as an artist, engineer, and physician-in-training. At the intersection of creativity and medicine, she hopes to use art as a tool to envision new treatment possibilities, educate patients and providers alike, and enhance the humanity of medicine.
